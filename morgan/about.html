About Me

My name is Sophie, and I'm currently a fourth-year Professional Writing and French Studies student. My digital authouring journey, like many others', is quite long and multifaceted. On the personal side of things, I enjoy digital authoring: using and posting on various social media platforms, like Reddit, Tumblr, YouTube, Twitter, and the like.

On the more "scholarly" side of things, I'm interested in digital authoring: how people digitally author and how they develop digital and coding literacy skills. In other words, my digital authoring journey and interests—in this course and beyond—seek to combine the practical and the theoretical to understand the possible via virtual and multimodal means.

You can also learn more about my digital authoring journey through this timeline!



About the Vision

I wanted the website to be a “virtual” or “cyber” space that readers can enter and experience and to evoke the same emotions and sensations (or “vibe”) as a dynamic "cyberspace." So, I aimed to create a somewhat experimental website that is more immersive and fluid, rather than guided and logical. In the end, I wanted to visualize cyberwriting through design choices and visual rhetoric so that the website is rhetorically parallel to my main message: humans and machines ultimately work in tandem to discover.
