Fandom Fiction

The Fandoms We Know and the Stories They Tell

Hi, I’m Jonell Ebreo, and this is Fandom Fiction. Here, we delve into the fascinating world of Fandom and Fanfiction. Fanfiction has existed as long as Fans have, no matter how far back you think they go. But we’re here to talk about and highlight modern fan communities or fandoms, the fanfictions they write, and the relationship fans have with them.

Here at Fandom Fiction, we select a few specific fanfictions, tropes, characters, or ships that have had a particular cultural effect on their fandom community, big or small. Whether it be the reception the fandom has of that story, how the fanfiction represents the fandom, or how the fanfiction has come to define the fandom itself.

Topics of Interest

Fanfiction

Fanfictions are written fanworks and stories that take a known property, like a TV show, book, movie, etc., and modify its story in some way. Despite worries about copyright, Fanfiction prove to be both an influential yet niche part of fandom culture.

Tropes

Tropes are certain story beats and patterns that are common in writing. Fandoms often have their own set of tropes that are well understood in fandom discourse and tend to refer to a broad set of terms, including Genre, Content, Character Archetypes, Storylines, and Motifs.

Characters

There are simply some characters that resonate with or change a fandom more than others. Sometimes it's is a major protagonist in a story, sometimes it’s a fan-friendly side character, sometimes it’s a random stock character that was seen once background.

Ships

Relationship pairings, or “Ships”, are a popular talking point amongst fans and fandoms. Simply put, a Ship is when two or more characters (and sometimes even an inanimate object) are put together into a romantic relationship. Most use abbreviated "Ship names."
