How I Use Archive of Our Own

Time Stamp: Audio: Visual:
0:00-0:03 *The song Sayera by Blue Dot Sessions begins playing* -Opening Title Card: “How I Use Archive of Our Own” appears before fading out.
0:03-0:08 Popular fanfiction website Archive of Our Own has recently hit 7 Million Fanworks. -Opening an announcement by Archive of Our Own announcing they recently hit 7 million fanworks.
0:08-0:14 That is a huge jump from their previous 5 million milestone only 2 years ago. -Opening the previous milestone announcement by Archive of Our Own when they had hit 5 million fanworks. I hover my cursor around the year, 2019, 2 years before the most recent milestone.
0:14-0:19 The Pandemic, like it or not, is definitely a major contributor to that jump… -Short clip of Doug Ford declaring Ontario in a State of Emergency during the Pandemic.
0:19-0:27 …with many new fanwriters and fanreaders joining AO3 and many old users coming back. -Video of someone (in this case, my little brother JP) searching up Archive of Our Own on a Computer.
0:27-0:31 I’m one of those old users. -A picture of Jonell, one of those old AO3 users. I am tired from worldbuilding for another story.
0:31-0:37 After a failed go at DeviantArt, I took a short break from actively writing fanfiction. -Scrolling through DeviantArt.
0:37-0:43 When the Pandemic hit, I decided to try and give it another go on AO3. -Fade transition.
-Now scrolling through AO3, emulating the same way I scrolled through DeviantArt.
0:43-0:57 Here I am figuring out what’s popular right now and putting my own twist on it. The Lumity ship from The Owl House is really popular right now, but that’s a lot of “hurt” content, so let’s throw some “fluff” in there. -I go through AO3 and scroll through some stories, picking out the Lumity ship from the cartoon The Owl House, but also hovering over the “hurt” tag.
-I open up Microsoft Word and start writing.
-The screen gets smaller and becomes a small box on the bottom left corner of the screen.
0:58-1:02 While other me’s doing that, let me tell you a bit about AO3. -Scrolling through AO3 again.
1:02-1:14 Tags are very important on AO3 as they’re the main way people are going to find your work. These tags include genre, fandom, characters, and content. -Screen zooms into the right-hand side of the display, getting a close up on the tag tools on the right-hand side of the website.
1:14-1:18 Lots of Fanfiction websites have a bad reputation for… -Different fanfiction website logos pop up. The logos are AO3, Fanfiction.net, and Wattpad.
1:18-1:20 … unsavory content. -An image of a woman looking at her phone in distress.
1:20-1:30 This is warranted of course, but there is good content to be found here. Which is why I use a blacklist tool to get through all of it called AO3rdr. Now I can block certain tags on AO3 and search through mostly without worry. -First shows more scrolling through AO3.
-Then opens my setting menu page for AO3rdr.
1:30-1:36 AO3 is great because you are usually required to label specific content warning on your stories. -Zooms in on the Warning filter of the AO3 on the right-hand side of AO3, circling it in red.
1:36-1:46 With these steps taken, you can now safely enjoy all the great content talented writers around the world have created for you. -More scrolling through AO3, even opening up a story.
1:46-1:50 Or… whatever the heck I just wrote. -The box that was writing in the bottom left hand corner now expands to full screen.
1:50-1:52 What the… -Zooms in on the words “Willuz for Life.”
-Willuz is the rival ship of Lumity.
1:52-1:53 *Music Cuts Out* -Screen Goes Black.
1:54-2:00 *Music Returns* -End Card: Picture of Jonell, asleep at a desk, holding a Guitar, wearing a The Owl House T-Shirt.
-Text: “Tired Jonell is Tired” and “The End.”
