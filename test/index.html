USABILITY RESEARCH PROJECT

Weight: 25% of final grade/250 points| Chapters: 2, 3, 4, 5

ASSIGNMENT OVERVIEW

This assignment applies design thinking phases by asking you to create a professional Usability Testing Plan for a digital product.

USABILITY TESTING PLAN (GROUP)

Weight: 15% (100 points group + 50 points individual) | Due: Feb. 1

What You're Creating

A complete plan to test a simple digital product (app, website, interface) with real users. Follows Chapter 4 methodologies (pages 60-76).

Step 1: Choose Your Product (Day 1-2)

Good examples: Food delivery app, university registration system, streaming service, e-commerce checkout
Requirements: Publicly accessible, task-oriented, testable in 30-45 minutes

Step 2: Research Your Target Audience (Days 3-6)

Everyone participates - spend 3-5 hours total as a team researching:

Secondary Research (required):

  • Read product's marketing materials and app store descriptions
  • Search: "[Product name] user demographics" and "[Product name] target audience"
  • Analyze 20-30 user reviews for patterns (who uses it, why, what frustrates them)
  • Find industry reports (Statista, Pew Research, Google Scholar)

Research Lead Tip: Compile findings into shared doc before writing this section.

Each person is primarily responsible for one section but everyone reviews and refines all sections.

Step 4: Collaborate via Google Docs (Days 4-12)

Project Manager creates shared Google Doc with section headings for each component.

Everyone:

  • Drafts their section by Jan. 25th
  • Reviews and comments on others’ sections (use "Suggesting" mode)
  • Responds to comments within 24 hours

Recommended Meetings or Communications

  • Group comm:Assign roles, choose product, set up doc
  • Meeting 1:Progress check, ensure alignment across sections
  • Meeting 2:Read aloud together, complete peer evaluations

Step 5: Submit (Feb. 1)

Project Manager submits via eClass

  1. Final Testing Plan (Google Doc) - 8-12 pages
  2. Google Doc link (set to edit)

Required Components

  1. Executive Summary- Product, research questions, target audience, goals
  2. Target Audience Definition- Demographics, experience level, recruitment criteria, sample size (cite your team’s research and use APA)
  3. Informed Consent- Adapt template from Chapter 4 (pp. 71-72): purpose, risks, benefits, confidentiality, voluntary participation
  4. Pre-Study Questions- Demographics (5-7), experience (3-5), technology use (2-3), , plus a 250-word rationale about these questions.
  5. Testing Protocol:
  • Instructions: Explains think-aloud, reassures participant product is being tested (not them)
  • Scenario: Realistic context motivating the tasks (see Ch. 4, p. 73 example)
  • Tasks (3-5): Prioritized with Success Completion Criteria for each
  1. Post-Study Questions- Overall experience (3-5), specific features (3-5), improvements, plus a 250-word rationale about these questions.
  2. Data Collection Plan- Recording methods, roles (facilitator, data logger), data management

YOUR ASSIGNED SECTION (15% of course grade) - INDIVIDUAL

Graded on your primary section only based on your assigned role.

EXCELLENT (90-100)

Section is professional-grade work. Follows Chapter 4 methodologies precisely. 

  • Executive summary: Provides clear overview of product, concise research question(s), brief target audience, and specific testing goals; synthesizes key elements from all sections; 1 page.
  • Target Audience:Clear demographics with cited research; justified sample size.
  • Informed Consent:Uses Ch. 4 template (pp. 71-72); all ethical elements present. 
  • Pre-Study Questions:Relevant, unbiased, clear rationale. 
  • Testing Protocol:Reassuring instructions; realistic scenario; 3-5 prioritized tasks with clear SCCs. 
  • Post-Study:Open-ended questions targeting key concerns. 
  • Data Collection:Methods, roles, ethical storage specified. Well-written, error-free, professional.

PROFICIENT (80-89)

Section complete and functional. Follows Ch. 4 guidelines. All required elements present. Clear and appropriate with minor gaps. Professional writing with minor errors. Usable with small revisions.

DEVELOPING (70-79)

Section present but lacks depth. Missing some elements or underdeveloped. Weak methodology or research. Errors or unclear language. Needs significant revision.

NEEDS IMPROVEMENT (Below 70)

Section incomplete or shows fundamental misunderstanding. Major elements missing. Doesn't follow Ch. 4 guidelines. Multiple errors. Not usable.

DOCUMENT COHESION (5% of course grade) - GROUP

How well the complete document works as a unified testing plan.

EXCELLENT (90-100)

All sections align seamlessly. Target audience consistent throughout. Tasks match audience goals. Questions address research objectives. Consistent terminology and tone. Document flows logically. Professional formatting. 8-12 pages. Could be used in actual research study.

PROFICIENT (80-89)

Sections work together with minor inconsistencies. Generally consistent terminology. Functional document with small alignment gaps. Professional overall.

DEVELOPING (70-79)

Noticeable inconsistencies between sections. Tasks don't fully match audience. Terminology varies. Sections feel disconnected. Formatting issues.

NEEDS IMPROVEMENT (Below 70)

Sections don't work together. Major inconsistencies or missing sections. Document unusable. Unprofessional.
