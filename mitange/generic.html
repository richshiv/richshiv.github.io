AUDIO TRANSCRIPT

[birds chirping in the background, music begins a few seconds later for transition]

The year 2018 was the year I plunged further into the world of digital media more than any year.

I remember it precisely because it was my first time posting on Instagram in years – it was for

prom by the way.Posting on Instagram wasn’t why it was so memorable, I realized that majority of my

unforgettable digital media experience came from that year. I created podcasts that year and shot and

edited more than two professional YouTube videos for school assignments. Diving into the

digital media world through school helped me see that there was so much freedom for creativity online.

It stirred a deep desire to be more creative with digital media and be productive with my imagination.

I can’t remember what gave me the idea, but sometime that year of 2018, I approached my mother, who’s

a pastry chef, and gave her an idea of opening her page. She had just begun pastry school, and she had a

home business, I thought “wouldn’t it be easier if you sold and offered your service through an online

platform?” She loved the idea, so I decided to build her website using WIX without any website development

experience. At that time, I had never heard of WordPress, WIX appeared more visually pleasing, but now in 2020,

I realize that WIX was very restricting. Nonetheless, I built the website added photos, but I didn’t have my

mothers permission to sell products because I didn’t know enough about how the payment system worked with WIX.

Fast-forward to 2019. A professor introduced me to WordPress, and I was very excited to start blogging that year

thrilled to explore a new digital media because WIX failed me.

[PAUSE]

It made me believe that I wasn’t capable of building a website because of my inexperience, but WordPress was different.

I didn’t know anything about coding, yet for every widget, WordPress offers a how-to tutorial. I especially

love the free templates provided it made my page look clean and unique without any payment. For many months that

was the only experience, I had concerning WordPress.

Finally, in the year 2020, I took part in a life-changing internship, the project due at the end of the program was “how to move

small businesses to an online platform during covid-19.” Everyone on my team carried similar or less experience than me, so we

struggled initially. However, I learned so much about website development. I learned that before creating any sort of media

platform one needs to strategize; we created a website called “Local Market.” This website was all about giving small businesses

a smooth transition to an online platform. I still don’t know how to code, but I learned to develop a marketing budget, customer

journey maps and a landing page all through the creation of Local Market. I also learned that to have a successful website one

needs to create traffic using SEO’s (search engine optimizations) such as google analytics. In fewer words, I learned that

there’s more to website development than pretty pictures clear writing. Finally, I realized that WordPress simply offers everything

I need to build a successful website. Yes like WIX, I still have to pay for premium access on WordPress; however, WordPress

offers various videos of how to get started to building a website, that even the least experienced, has a chance on building a

successful website.
