Feature: Breaking Convention: Physical Thinking with William Forsythe

May 2019 /feature essay for NBoC's live performance print program and Ballet+ online platform


Excerpt: “Approximate Sonata 2016 wraps precision in a laissez faire attitude. It’s a deceptively casual series of linked duets, with movement that rapidly accumulates and layers like a multi-exposure photograph. Once again working with Thom Willems, Forsythe asks dancers to skim through unpredictable technical challenges as the music morphs in mood and tempo... In all three works, Forsythe combines an enthusiasm for legacy elements such as pointe work with radical phrasing and pace, torqued shapes and an absence of the gender specificities of many ballets, both traditional and contemporary.”

Excerpt: “Most image generators work in the same general way. Essay: Migrating Meaning

March 2023/ Collection Regards Hybrides

In addition to serving on the programming committee for Collection Regards Hybrides archive of dance on film and video, Kathleen contributed an online essay that explores the work of filmmaker and artist Laura Taler.

Excerpt: “In much of Taler’s work, split emotional allegiances and a sense of displacement—the kind that remains long after settling into a new environment—evoke some of the instabilities of the immigrant experience... Eastern European heritage resonates in most of her work, tangentially with titling, settings, cos- tuming, and objects, or more specifically in location, song, and folk dance, as in the village trilogy and other dance and film works.”

Feature: The Evolving Story of Dance on Film

May 2018 / Dance International print and online

Excerpt: "Evann Siebens, an ex-dancer and accomplished cameraperson, rode the dancefilm wave in the ’90s. Fiction: Boreal

2022 / short story

Excerpt:"The map maker labours long into the night tracing the edge of the Boreal on the ancient stereo plotter, then gathers pens to ink the topography. Pale green like the underbelly of a woodfern in spring for marshes, blue as a summer sky for hills, magenta for the hardscape infrastructure installed by the province. He notes that the boundaries have shrunk yet again. This must be his third major update in a decade. The map maker remembers the original survey, walking the Manito Miikana trail with Edie, the views of the Pic River and its dunes to the North, glimpses of Lake Superior to the west. He had surveyed most of the great unbroken swathe of the boreal forest; back then it ran from the Labrador coast to the Alaskan out islands dotting the Bering Sea."

About Kathleen

Kathleen Smith is a Toronto-based writer and web designer with an interest in the arts. She has worked as a performer, producer, curator and creative director, and is the recipient of numerous arts grants for personal projects, and as a member of the dance+words collective.

Kathleen’s written work spans many forms, both print and digital, creative and critical. In legacy print media, she has written for the Globe and Mail, the Toronto Star, NOW Weekly, Toronto Life Fashion, House and Home, Azure, and many other national and international publications. From 2011 to 2014, she was editor-in-chief of The Dance Current, Canada’s dance magazine. On diverse digital platforms, including dance international, pointe and tanz, Kathleen continues to investigate issues in performance and culture.

Kathleen also has experience grant-writing, copy writing and creating marketing and promotional materials and white papers for a range of clients in the non-profit and cultural sectors. She is a long-standing Wikipedia contributor and editor and hosts regular write and edit-a-thons for arts communities across Canada. In scholarly works, she is contributing to an upcoming anthology about dance on film to be published by Routledge in 2024.

Kathleen regularly experiments with fiction and creative non-fiction and is especially interested in the intersections between the two.
