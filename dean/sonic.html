Sonic Essay

Dean Wardak · Sonic Essay

(Piano and drums start playing) Welcome to Dean Wardak’s sonic essay. I use a variety of technologies to complete different tasks for digital authoring required for my motivational speaking. I will be focusing on a talk I did for the Brain Injury Association of Peel-Halton last September. For the talk I did for BIAPH, I used the Zoom app on my iPad to give the talk to my audience. I was planning to use the Keynote app too to use a PowerPoint presentation I created to add a powerful visual aspect during the talk, but I ran into some friction with Zoom and the host of the meeting.

(Piano tone changes)

I will start the sonic essay with the creation of my PowerPoint presentation I use during some of my talks using the Keynote app. I want my talks to be powerful and leave a long-lasting impression on my audience. My PowerPoint is a visual narrative of who I was before the accident, the accident and my journey after the accident. I incorporate images of myself before the accident on one slide, graphic images of the wrecked car on the next slide then a picture of myself in a coma while on life-support to show the severity of my injuries on the next slide. I also incorporate pictures and videos of my successes after my accident to inspire the audience on the next sequence of slides. The slideshow in combination with my words create a powerful synchronization of audio and visual stimulation for my audience.

(Piano tone changes)

Prior to my talk with BIAPH, I practiced giving the presentation while having the PowerPoint presentation open on my iPad. I would practice voice intonation, memorization and transitions. To help make my talk effective, I plan out the perfect timing and word choice to change the slides on the PowerPoint presentation. This makes for a smooth and easy to follow talk while maximizing the effects of the visuals from the PowerPoint and audio from my words.

(Piano tone changes)

On the day of the actual talk, I used the link sent to me by the host to join the Zoom meeting 15 minutes before the start time. I joined early to make sure everything for the presentation was working properly. In order for me to use my PowerPoint presentation though, I needed the host to make me a cohost to allow me to share my screen. Unfortunately, the host was not able to make me a cohost, and I could not upload the PowerPoint directly to the Zoom meeting or email the presentation to the host because of the video in the PowerPoint requiring the host to have the video file on their computer.

Luckily, since I memorized the words I have to say prior to the presentation, I opted to give the talk without the PowerPoint. (Piano stops) (applause sound effect starts) It was still a good talk, and I received phenomenal feedback, but the PowerPoint not being available took away from the effects the pictures and video could of had on the audience.

Thanks for listening to my sonic essay. (Applause and drums stop)
